The Problem with Self-Learning

Self-learning is difficult. Students are challenged to sift through dozens of sites that promise to teach them how to code. While the resources are out there, the content is fragmented.

Students waste time and energy attempting to assemble their own curriculum, often ending up with an incomplete understanding of the programming language as well as underdeveloped skills in debugging and engineering workflow.

These students struggle with difficult questions in interviews because they lack the strong foundations required to navigate through any arbitrary problem.
