The Problem with Self-Learning

Self-learning is hard. Students are challenged to sift through dozens of sites that promise to teach them how to code. While the resources are out there, the content is fragmented.

Students waste time and energy attempting to assemble their own curriculum, often ending up with an incomplete understanding of the programming language as well as underdeveloped skills in debugging and engineering workflow.

These students struggle with difficult questions in interviews because they lack the strong foundations required to navigate through any arbitrary problem.

Curriculum Based on Solid Fundamentals

Fulcrum is a carefully designed to help students construct a robust mental model of how programming languages work, and a set of debugging processes to isolate problems when code doesn’t work.

The Fulcrum curriculum has evolved from many years of working with talented students like you. Not only have the concepts been scrutinized for accuracy and preciseness, the order in which they’re presented has been meticulously arranged to steadily build upon and reinforce the previous learnings.
